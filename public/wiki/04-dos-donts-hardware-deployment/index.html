Do’s and Don’ts of Hardware Deployment

Loading and Unloading Equipment

Best Practices

  • Always lift with your legs, not your back
  • Use two people for heavy equipment
  • Secure all items before transport
  • Keep pathways clear

Gaffers Tape vs. Duct Tape

Gaffers Tape

  • Purpose: Securing cables to floors and surfaces
  • Key feature: Leaves NO sticky residue when removed
  • Use case: Floor cable runs, temporary installations

Duct Tape

  • Purpose: General purpose sealing and repair
  • Key feature: Strong adhesive, leaves residue
  • Use case: DO NOT use for cable management on floors

IMPORTANT: Only use Gaffers tape on the conference floor. Duct tape leaves sticky residue that is difficult to clean up and can damage venue surfaces.

Purpose of Tape

Tape Type Use Case Residue
Gaffers Tape Floor cables, temporary marking No
Duct Tape Repairs, heavy duty (not floor) Yes

Clean Installation and Removal Protocol

Installation

  1. Plan cable routes before running cables
  2. Use appropriate cable lengths (avoid excess slack)
  3. Secure cables with gaffers tape every 6-8 feet
  4. Label both ends of all cables
  5. Document all connections

Removal

  1. Remove all tape carefully
  2. Roll up cables properly (see wrapping guide)
  3. Check for damage and document
  4. Return cables to proper storage
  5. Clean up any residue left behind

Difference in Cables

Network Cables (Ethernet)

  • Cat5e: Up to 1 Gbps, 100m max
  • Cat6: Up to 10 Gbps (short distances), 100m max
  • Cat6a: Up to 10 Gbps, 100m max, thicker

Power Cords

  • Check voltage requirements
  • Use only provided power cables
  • Don’t daisy-chain power strips

Fiber Optics

  • Handle with care - sensitive to bending
  • Use proper connectors
  • Never look directly into fiber ends

Basic Loop Technique

  1. Start with the connector in your hand
  2. Create loops of 4-6 inches in diameter
  3. Maintain consistent loop size
  4. Finish with connector at the end of the coil
  5. Secure with velcro tie or loose twist tie

Coiling Best Practices

  • Always coil in the same direction
  • Never twist or kink the cable
  • Keep loops loose - don’t over-tighten
  • Store with connectors protected
